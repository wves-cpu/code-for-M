Recovery Methods

Science-Backed Techniques

🍅

Pomodoro Method

Work in focused 25-min sprints with 5-min breaks. After 4 rounds, take a 30-min rest.

25:00 ready
🌊

Ultradian Rhythms

Your brain cycles through 90-min performance peaks. Work with them, not against them.

Work 90 min → Rest 20 min
📵

Digital Detox Hours

Designate 2 hours daily with zero screens. Your brain needs full offline recovery time.

Recommended: 7–9 PM
🧊

Cold Exposure

Brief cold showers release norepinephrine — a natural mood elevator with lasting effects.

Start: 30 sec cold shower
🌅

Morning Sunlight

10 minutes of morning sun sets your circadian rhythm and improves sleep quality by 50%.

Best: Within 30 min of waking
✍️

Gratitude Practice

3 specific things daily trains your brain to notice positives, reducing stress reactivity.

Takes only 5 minutes

Advice Library

Recovery Tips by Category

Set Hard Stop Times

Define a non-negotiable end time. Close your laptop, physically leave your workspace.

Practice Saying No

Every yes to something unimportant is a no to what matters. Protect your energy fiercely.

Single-Tasking

Multitasking reduces productivity by 40%. Focus on one thing until completion.

Consistent Schedule

Go to bed and wake at the same time daily — even weekends. Consistency is the key.

Dark & Cool Room

Temperature of 65–68°F (18–20°C) and complete darkness optimize deep sleep quality.

No Screens Before Bed

Blue light suppresses melatonin. Stop all screens 90 minutes before sleep time.

Zone 2 Cardio

150 min/week of light cardio (still able to talk) transforms mitochondria and energy levels.

Anti-Inflammatory Diet

Reduce processed sugar and seed oils. Add omega-3s, leafy greens, and fermented foods.

Daily Stretching

10 minutes of full-body stretching reduces cortisol and strengthens body-mind connection.

Daily Meditation

Even 5 minutes of breath focus rewires stress pathways in the prefrontal cortex.

Evening Journaling

Write 3 wins and 1 lesson every night. This reframes your day positively before sleep.

Read Fiction

Reading fiction increases empathy and provides cognitive escape that screens cannot replicate.
